A Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model estimates the intrinsic value of a company based on the estimated future cash flow it is projected to generate. This project uses SEC filings to build a five year DCF model for The Coca-Cola Company. I used three years of data from the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement to derive projection assumptions including a 3% revenue growth rate, 25% EBIT margin, and estimates for D&A, CapEx, and net working capital as a percentage of revenue. I used these values to calculate a weighted average cost of capital (WACC) of 5.5% using the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) for cost of equity and an implied cost of debt from historical interest expense. I then projected five years of unlevered free cash flow (UFCF), discounted each year's cash flows to present value, and estimated a terminal value assuming a 2.5% perpetuity growth rate. Lastly, I used the enterprise value to determine the equity value and an implied share price of $61.03.
